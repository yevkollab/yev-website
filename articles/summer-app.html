2025 summer app

September 1, 2025

I (almost) finished my first app! It was surprisingly not that simple for me and while app is going through apple review I want to document my process in this article.

Idea

Ideation were somehow the hardest part for me. Usually I like when things are going organically, like 'I had a problem - I solved it - turns out other people have the same problem' or when I started my first company I didn't think twice about is it good or do I be able to do it, I just had a feeling 'alright it will be fun and I want to do it now'

The things a little different now since I don't have a clear quest yet and don't know what I wanna do with life except 'would be cool to build successful b2c startup'

So, idea search took me more than 2 weeks.

I started with the things I like the most, than I tried research the thigs I know a lot about, and in the end I stopped in not so popular niche with 2 competitors (one of those a famous twitter influencer)

I know nothing about this niche but the numbers and competitors tells me I have quite a good chances to earn some mrr next couple of month.

Idk, I always choose things I like rather than good numbers, but nothing bad will be out of just trying something new?

Design UI and UX

The most enjoyable phase of building for me is always design. Took me 2 weeks to design 21 screens include onboarding. (will share some soon!)

My design process is:

  1. Research (UX)
  2. Feature list
  3. Wireframe in Figma
  4. UI

While UI is straightforward - approach to research changed for me this year chat did a good job to help me understand my users, like,

what they do
what their value
what they like
etc

Since I have no idea about the niche, it was awesome starting point. Second source was a reddit, which is also good to study the audience and what they like.

After the UX research I just write down the features and designed everything else in Figma

What's Next

One good thing about being not in the hurry - I don't have a high expectation that my first ios app will achieve the 10k mrr. I have quite a lot time to do exactly same process time and time again which I sure will be doing.

You see, I reflected a lot on my past experience and I have a feeling that it will be really hard for me to be employed because I spent 5 years building my own business - I need at least 10k/month but I'm not sure I can do something useful for other (I can do everything at the okay level)

This makes me very bad candidate for employment (I don't even know the position I potentially can apply)

I know I need to be better in sales and English to achieve 10k and with working app in the apple store I will now have a great opportunity to study both the same time.
